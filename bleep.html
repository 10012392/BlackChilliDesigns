Bleep

Sally and the team at Tuskany Agency worked with Terry at EOL and created a character, a retro robot called Bleep, to be a part of EOL's internet package.

Terry and EOL have for several years been involved with the sponsors of a specific scientific group called the Earth to Sky Calculus, based in the USA and headed by Dr Phillips. They work with students to conduct experiments at 100,000+ feet, where the earth's atmosphere touches space, collecting the data with weather balloons.

The experiments habe been conducted in many US states and all over the world, thanks to Terry and EOL for the first time in New Zealand.

Along with the science equitment and a GPS tracker the balloon can take a payload and also carry a camera which means the whole 2 - 3 hour trip to space and back is recorded.

EOL wanted one of the payloads to be their mascot Bleep, but there was a problem, Bleep was a graphic design. So the task was to use their images to bring Bleep to life as a 3D robot. He would then be mounted on a platform, fixed to the rails of the balloon and fly to space and back.
